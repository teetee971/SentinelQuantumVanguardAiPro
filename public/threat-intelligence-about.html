Executive Summary

The Global Threat Intelligence module is a frontend-only visualization platform designed to provide situational awareness of worldwide cyber threat patterns using publicly available OSINT (Open Source Intelligence) data.

This tool is purpose-built for institutional environments where audit compliance, data privacy, and transparent methodology are paramount. It serves educational and analytical purposes without performing any active security functions or collecting user data.

Official SOC & Institutional Use Cases

  • 1. Security Operations Center (SOC) Displays Visualization for SOC wall displays showing global threat landscape trends. Provides real-time situational awareness without exposing sensitive organizational data.
  • 2. Executive & Board Briefings Clear, visual presentations of cyber threat trends for C-level executives and board members. Explainable insights based on transparent OSINT sources without technical jargon.
  • 3. Audit & Compliance Demonstrations Demonstrate security awareness capabilities to auditors and compliance officers. All functionality is transparent, documented, and audit-safe with no hidden operations.
  • 4. Cybersecurity Training & Education Training scenarios for SOC analysts, security teams, and students. Educational tool for understanding threat patterns, geographic distribution, and attack vectors.
  • 5. Public Demonstrations & Conferences Safe for public demonstrations at conferences, trade shows, and institutional events. No sensitive data, no proprietary algorithms, no operational capabilities exposed.
  • 6. Institutional Research & Analysis Academic and research institutions can use aggregated threat data for studying cyber threat trends, patterns, and geographic distributions at a macro level.

Data Sources Transparency

All threat data is sourced from publicly available OSINT feeds including:

  • Public Honeypot Networks: Community-driven threat sensors capturing attack patterns
  • Threat Intelligence Feeds: Publicly shared threat indicators and attack signatures
  • CVE & Vulnerability Databases: Publicly disclosed security vulnerabilities
  • Security Research Publications: Academic and industry research on threat trends
  • DNS & Network Telemetry: Publicly available network infrastructure observations

Aggregation & Privacy Methodology

All threat indicators are aggregated at the country level. Individual IP addresses, organizations, or personally identifiable information are never collected, stored, or displayed. The visualization represents general threat patterns and trends based on public data sources, suitable for strategic analysis but not operational incident response.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can this tool detect threats on our network?

No. This is a visualization platform only. It displays aggregated public OSINT data and does not scan, monitor, or analyze any specific networks, devices, or systems.

Does it collect any data about our organization?

No. The platform collects zero data. It runs entirely in your browser with no backend, no cookies, no tracking, and no telemetry. Network inspection will confirm zero outbound connections.

Is it compliant with GDPR and data protection regulations?

Yes. No personal data is collected, processed, or stored. All displayed data is aggregated at country level from public sources, making it inherently compliant with privacy regulations.

Can we use it in air-gapped or restricted environments?

Yes. The platform is entirely frontend-based with no external dependencies, APIs, or required internet connections beyond loading the initial page. It can be deployed in isolated environments.

How do auditors verify there's no hidden functionality?

Code Review: All code is clean, commented, and readable. Network monitoring shows zero backend communications. Browser developer tools can inspect all operations. The architecture is designed to be fully transparent and auditable.

What's the difference between this and operational threat intelligence platforms?

Operational platforms perform active detection, analysis, and response to specific threats targeting an organization. This platform provides general situational awareness of global threat patterns for education and strategic understanding, not operational security.
